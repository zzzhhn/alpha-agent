Factor Acceptance Criteria

Each candidate factor is evaluated against the following thresholds. A factor must pass all gate conditions to be accepted into the registry.

IC Gate
|IC Mean| > 0.02 — The factor must show statistically meaningful cross-sectional correlation with next-day forward returns. Below this threshold, the signal is indistinguishable from noise after transaction costs.
Consistency Gate
|ICIR| > 0.5 — The Information Coefficient must be consistent over time (IC mean / IC std). A high IC with low ICIR suggests the alpha is concentrated in a few lucky periods, not a durable edge.
Profitability Gate
Sharpe > 0.5 — The long-short quintile portfolio (top 20% long, bottom 20% short, equal-weighted, daily rebalance) must deliver risk-adjusted returns above the Sharpe 0.5 bar after accounting for turnover.
Decay Check
IC(t+3) > 50% of IC(t+1) — Alpha must persist for at least 3 trading days. Factors whose IC collapses within 1 day are too expensive to trade due to execution slippage and microstructure effects.
